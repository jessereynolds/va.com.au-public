ONLINE AND CLICKING 
With so much going on in the noise festival during the month of October, there's one place you can come to get an overview of it all - noise.net.au. Over 10,000 artists, writers, photographers, filmmakers, print and web designers, industrial, interior and fashion designers, musicians, animators and many more clever types have submitted material for us to feast our senses on, and right here is the best place to begin your festival experience. 
We're cataloguing and displaying the best from all submissions, profiling artists and linking to the many tasty chasms of cyberspace that the nation's under-25s have made their own. At the same time, we'll be keeping the people up to speed with the daily noise happenings in print, TV, radio

"In the DIY age many young Australians have taken the publishing of their own work into their own hands - creating, sharing, communicating and publishing their homemade works on their own websites, with no restrictions or corporate philosophies to follow," says Online Producer Millie Lawson. "The noise site aims to provide an exciting common forum for all of these people."

In collaboration with our media partners, noise.net.au presents the work of Australia's young visionaries in the form of dedicated homepages, artist profiles and expansive galleries. Using the dynamic and evolving medium of the web, noise.net.au is a user-friendly and exciting space designed to explore and exhibit the creative opportunities offered by the online domain.

Here's the rundown of what's happening and where to find it:
E-works // displaying the best from our open-slather invitation for net heads to show what they do, from web layout, content and design to interactive and games
Online Gallery // our online image space, including a digital art collection in our online gallery by Sydney's Museum Of Contemporary Art
Flash Animations // we house the best from our wierd and wonderful Flash submissions
Postcards // our gallery of the coolest Avant Card designs by young, noisy Australians
Snapshot // the online gallery of Kodak's disposable camera project, which also resulted in a superb book.
Fake Ads // advertisements about fake brands and slogans as made by noise entrants.

Make your first stop the Festival Projects page - the gateway to the vast amount of talent involved in noise, and the avenue to all other projects that artists could submit works to. Still don't know what the hell noise is all about? Go here. Enjoy...
