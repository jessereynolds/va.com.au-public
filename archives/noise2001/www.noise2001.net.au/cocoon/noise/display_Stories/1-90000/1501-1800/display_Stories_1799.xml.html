HIT ME, BABY, ONE MORE TIME 
As far as the teen press goes, magazines don't get much bigger, brighter or more brash than TV Hits. The publication reaches well over 100,000 young readers - making it the perfect vehicle for some noisy, adolescent, Aussie artwork. 
Here at the noise festival we launched a project call-out for TV Hits magazine only a couple of months before the festival's launch date - and the response, despite that last-minute call-out, was enormous.

"Basically we asked for anything that could sit on the pages of a magazine," explains Tracey Grimson, print producer for the festival. "And what we received was about as broad as you would expect - from wildly colourful artwork such as the gaming and fantasy inspired drawings of Jade Denton through to your garden-variety teen angst poetry."

Not that there&sbquo;s anything wrong with that. After all, if anyone's allowed to vent their frustrations with a rhyming couplet who better than a hormonally raging teen?

All of the submitted work was sent through to the TV Hits editorial team, headed up by larger-than-life editor Stu McCarney, who then selected a handful of works to be featured in the mag's November 2001 issue (on newsstands during October).

The work which eventually made it onto the printed page was, according to Stu, chosen for how engaging it would be for the "typical" TV Hits reader - who's generally a young music fan with an eye for the next big thing. One of the next big things chosen was the collage work of Jason Hoeung who submitted a clever look at mass culture cloning. For the music fans there's some fan art afoot - Chris Phillips' homage to guitarist Wes Borland.

On top of that, naturally the work had to fit into the TV Hits design mould, which is bright, sharp and attention-grabbing. The luminous photographs of Chloe nicolet-dit-felix with their brilliant colours and funky angles will feel right at home on the pages.

Fortunately, the noise festival is overflowing with bright, sharp and attention-grabbing young creative types. You gotta love a happy marriage!
