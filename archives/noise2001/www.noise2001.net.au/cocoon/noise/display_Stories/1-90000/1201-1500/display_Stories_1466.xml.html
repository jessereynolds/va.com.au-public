NAVA GETS NOISY 
Okay, so young artists don't get a lot of credit and may not be part of the highest income bracket in the country - hell, perhaps not even in an income bracket. But we think that's all the more reason to celebrate and salute them in October. noise and the National Association for the Visual Arts (NAVA) are turning the spotlight on the work of young visual and craft artists through the online initiative An Artist A Day. 
Via the visualarts.net.au site, NAVA is profiling 31 artists aged 25 or under, as a way of celebrating the incredible value and diversity of the younger end of the arts community.

"This project has unearthed an eclectic group of enthusiastic young visual artists," says noise Special Projects Manager Krissie Scudds." In publicly profiling these artists, NAVA and noise are blowing apart old misconceptions of what visual arts is all about."

It's interesting to see the reinvention of the 'artist' through this new crop of youngbloods expressing themselves through creative writing, photography, music journalism, web design, animation, performance, airbrushing, drawing, design, painting, printmaking, new media, book creation and much, much more.

They have one thing in common - they're all creative souls working hard to express themselves in a diverse range of media and forms. And as part of this online venture they now step up onto the digital stage to share their work with Australia and the world.

NAVA's Executive Director Tamara Winikoff says, "While many young digital artists already access the web to display their work, An Artist A Day opens up the online arena to young people involved in all areas of visual arts and craft practice."

Being such an art-appreciative bunch here at noise we couldn't help but want to profile these artists - after all, they are the future of modern culture. To find out more about this great initiative, head to visualarts.net.au.
