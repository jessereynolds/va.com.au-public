noise: DOING IT AUSSIE STYLE 
~ Everybody was trying to create something raw and individual - not borrowed from overseas designers ~ Our country's hippest and most stylish magazine just got noisier, with an exclusive supplement featuring fashions and designs from our hottest young creatives. 
And as you can see from Australian Style Fashion and Beauty Director Mimi Xu's comment, a new generation of Australian style might well be at our doorstep.

"Projects like the noise supplement are very valuable for young talent to get their work noticed, but also to encourage creativity," acknowledges Xu. "Lots of people have the talent but don't have the connections to be known by the wider public - this is a good way of letting them express themselves and share their vision with the readers."

To participate in the project, young Australians with outstanding fashion or accessory design concepts were asked to submit their works to nnoise, with some of the finalists even having their outfits professionally styled, modelled, photographed and published in October's edition of Australian Style.

One such well-earned contender is Anna Moretti, whose rough-around-the-edges clothing designs make no effort to conceal zippers, hems, folds, tucks or loose bits of fabric, but rather accentuates them in deliciously unconventional ways.

In stark contrast are Anna's delicate handbag series, complete with floral embroidery and detailed ribbon, cord and fringing additions. "I would describe my work as quite minimalist, simple, elegant, clean, and incorporating as much negative space and sans serif type as I can get," Anna explains.

Similarly, the DIY/punk methods of Sally Hulse see a designer stretching the boundaries of fashion by letting her love of '80s music and time spent in New York inspire her eclectic range of denim corsages, leather-tasselled neckpieces and the like. Think Madonna, Bon Jovi and Duran Duran.

A common thread of diversity features in all artist works throughout the supplement, from Johanna Guerin's hat-making business and Scarlett Gowing's unconventional dresses and daywear to the brilliant T-shirt designs of Eyal Bernard whose works are causing a bit of a ruckus in Parisian fashion circles.

A breath of fresh air indeed, and a project where raw energy marries tidily with the big name chic you've already come to expect from Australian Style.
"For a magazine to operate you can't only talk about new, unknown creatives," Mimi Xu says," but it is very valuable for us to showcase young talent amongst the more established features."
