SCHOOLS IN

Finding myself with some free time on a weekday, I decide to travel out to the nearest university and recapture some sense of my recently forgotten youth. Walking around the campus is a lovely experience, reading the noticeboards and taking in the atmosphere of expectant and privileged youth, but, this feeling soon evaporates as I come across a bunch of students eating lunch.

They are not overt in their staring, but I feel very out of place. Quite naked in fact, as they laugh and talk loudly. I look at my reflection in a plate glass window and realise that, not wearing a backpack, trainers, or a mobile phone make me look like a complete impostor.

So, to rescue the situation, I ingeniously take a milk crate from outside the union cafe and stand on it to begin an impromptu performance of my never-finished thesis, on the dada movement and contemporary politics. Also, I throw in a few references to daytime soap opera television, and the disenfranchisement of young people with fat trust funds but nowhere to spend the cash, and finish with a poem and some free interpretive dance.

This seems to do the trick, and some men, all dressed in long black coats, invite me to the bar to talk about their club, the 'internet porn for study-related purposes' society. Just like the old days...
