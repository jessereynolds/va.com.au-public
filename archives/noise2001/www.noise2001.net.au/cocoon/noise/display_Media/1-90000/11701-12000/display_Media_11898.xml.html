MOMENTS AND NOISES

Come on, come on, come on. Only a few seconds to go now. 1-3-5. Fingers all in the right places. I have to begin. It's all up to me to begin. I look up to see their faces. I'll explain to you later what I saw at that moment because I bet if I told you now you'd scoff with disbelief. But enough of that. Must concentrate. They nodded. This is the moment. Ready. Go.

So out from the 20-foot speakers on each side of the stage came the orchestra strings, a deep and haunting aroma of sound that made everybody who heard, even from hundreds of metres away, stop, draw breath and turn.

A minor. C major, two, three, four, breathe. It was sounding okay. I was doing okay. People were smiling. People were looking thoughtful. Nobody was booing or shouting at my musical inadequacies.
So relax, willya?

So I did. The singers began their chant. I didn't understand what they were singing, but there was no need for translation into mere English. The impact of the noise was more than enough.

The noise drew people to tears. The noise represented why the East Timorese kept struggling for independence for so many years. The noise answered all the questions plaguing these troops from Australia, Jordan, Kenya, wherever else, of why they left their homes far away to be brought here. The noise forgot politics. It was a noise for the people, too personal to trivialise.

I can't recall much after that moment, the day I played the opening chords of the Tour Of Duty Christmas Concert for the Troops in East Timor. There are flashes of this and that, like blurry photographs. Maybe it was the nerves. Maybe it's my rational and well-pampered mind disbelieving my memory's fantastical images. But I can still remember everything I'd heard, and all the impact that the sounds and music imprinted on my soul. The noise of the keys beneath my fingers, the silence of the gut-wrenched stadium, the wafts of laughter between Roy & HG's jokes, even the noise of the crowd when they bellowed 'no way, get fucked, fuck off!' to Doc Neeson (didn't broadcast that one). And finally the noise of 'goodbye'.

music = sound = noise
peace = truth = deliverance
