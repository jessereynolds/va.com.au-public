name  // Music In the Sticks, 'Live Ranges' Project, 3 RAM. 
creator  // Anna Poletti 
project  //COMMUNITY RADIO 
'Live Ranges' is a project to record several young local bands in the Macedon Ranges in Victoria.

The project is to record the bands performing their original material, at a live gig, and place two tracks per band on a live compilation album to be released.

The album will then be profiled on 3 RAM (100.7) - VOICE OF THE RANGES (local community radio station) through the weekly youth program, and other programs.

The 'Live Ranges' CD will be released independently by Music In The Sticks, which is a FReeZA funded youth committee. MITS has been successfully running music and other drug and alcohol free events in the Macedon Ranges Shire for the past 4 years. Run by and for young people in the region. The MITS members have been training with 3 RAM to become qualified as presenters / producers of the weekly youth program on the station.

The aim of the project is to give young local bands the opportunity to have their work professionally recorded and released, and have significant air-play in the local area. The project will also provide local bands with high-quality material that they may then use as a demo when approaching managers, or venues for gigs.


The 'Live Ranges" Project CD will be featured on community radio as part of noise and Amrap's community radio project. You can catch the CD features on 3 RAM as well as on the community radio satellite network.

noise has joined forces with Amrap - the Australian music radio airplay project, (a Commonwealth Government initiative that promotes and supports the significant role that community radio stations around the country play in supporting, developing, recording and broadcasting Australian music) to fund the production of a whole swag of new music programs and recordings. All the shows have been made by young community radio producers, 25 and under, about young Australian musos - who are also all 25 and under.

The range of music styles covered in the programs reflect the incredible diversity that exists on community radio throughout the country - from punk to country, classical to post-rock - with heaps in between.

The shows are being broadcast on community radio stations across the country by the producing stations as well as on the community radio satellite network - tune in for live band recordings, interviews with young artists and great new music.
