project  //COMMUNITY RADIO 
I'm making a radio program called 'South Coast Sounds" for noise.

"South Coast Sounds" consists of interviews and recordings of a few of the Far South Coasts youth performers - both solo musicians and bands.

The recording was done live in a small venue called the Murrah hall, which is a popular venue for many big bands that come to the far south coast.

When I selected the bands for my program I tried to show a different style of music from a variety of South Coast towns. Which in most cases was very easy to do because in each different town there is a different dominant style of music played.

All the performers in the program are playing original pieces that they wrote themselves, most of the performers are under 18 the youngest being just ten years of age. He played his first payed gig at age six and wasn't a bad drummer either

A big thank you goes out to all the performers and also of course to John McVeity for recording and mixing the whole event.


Indi's radio program is being broadcast on community radio as part of noise and Amrap's community radio project. You can catch Indi's programs on 93.7 EDGE FM (2BAR) as well as on the community radio satellite network.

noise has joined forces with Amrap - the Australian music radio airplay project, (a Commonwealth Government initiative that promotes and supports the significant role that community radio stations around the country play in supporting, developing, recording and broadcasting Australian music) - to fund the production of a whole swag of new music programs and recordings. All the shows have been made by young community radio producers, 25 and under, about young Australian musos - who are also all 25 and under.

The range of music styles covered in the programs reflect the incredible diversity that exists on community radio throughout the country - from punk to country, classical to post-rock - with heaps in between.

The shows are being broadcast on community radio stations across the country by the producing stations as well as on the community radio satellite network - tune in for live band recordings, interviews with young artists and great new music.
