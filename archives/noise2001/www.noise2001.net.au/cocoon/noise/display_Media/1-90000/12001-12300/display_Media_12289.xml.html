Epicure's Juan Alban gives noise the rundown on 'Under Your Radar':

Can you tell us about the inspiration for the track?//

With 'Under Your Radar' we wanted it to have that 'road song' quality. The kind of song you put on a compilation tape when your'e going on a road trip.

Lyrically, the inspiration for this song came from being in two minds about something. Kind of a 'should I stay or should I go?' scenario.

Were there any particular production techniques used for the recording of the track?//

We used fairly conventonal production techniques for this song, although we experimented a bit with different instruments and backing vocals until we were happy with the end result.

What type of equipment did you use?//

Apart from the standard drums/bass/guitar and vocal mix we usually use, we incorporated a hammond organ into the song. We also tried a number of different guitars to get that 'warm' quality we thought the song needed, eventually using a Telecaster and Gibson SG.

Can tell us your favourite local artists?//

In Epicure we've all got pretty different taste in music but some of the local artists we all love include You Am I, The Dead Salesmen, Clann Zu, The Cruel Sea, The Superjesus and The Rye Catchers.

So, who are your favourite artists of all time?//

Some of our favourite artists of all time would include Ben Harper, Beck, Nick Drake, Pearl Jam, R.E.M, They Might Be Giants, Radiohead, Jeff Buckley, Faith No More, Fiona Apple, Non-Intentional Lifeform and Turquoise.

Any tips for people undertaking their first recording session?//

I guess the best tip we could give to people undertaking their first recording session is to know what you want for each song and be well rehearsed before you go into the studio.

Did you ultimately achieve the result you wanted?//

We think 'Under Your Radar' is a great song to drive to, so in that respect we achieved our vision for the song. Of our recent recordings this is probably the song that we were the most satisfied with.

What is your ultimate goal and/or aspiration?//

I guess our ultimate goal for Epicure is to still be proud of our music in twenty years time and not have any regrets when it all ends. As songwriters I think the ultimate aspiration is to write a song that really means something someone and will always represent something special to that person.

Is there anything else that you would like to mention?//

The only thing we'd like to mention is that you can find 'Under Your Radar'on our latest EP 'ELEVATOR' out now thru MGM Distribution.


Epicure's 'Under Your Radar' can also be heard on the Making noise CD - free with the October's Rolling Stone magazine.
