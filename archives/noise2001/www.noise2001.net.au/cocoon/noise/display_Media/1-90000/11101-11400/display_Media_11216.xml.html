I've always been into drawing, mainly Star Wars and robots that kind of thing.

Old school anime like Astro-boy and Battle of The Planets, then Transformers and Robotech perked up my intrest in animation later on. Drawing came naturally I suppose but it wasn't until later in my teens when it became more of a "concious" passion and I used my abilty to illustrate my headspace at the time and how I related to the world around me. That's when I let my art become more personal.

I'd like to finish off this shareware action/ adventure game I'm creating with an absolute bloody genius (heya Monty!) and see where it goes. I'd also like to get into broadcast a bit more and realise some short animation ideas I've had floating around. I love the moving image and the combination of great audio, visuals and interactivity really turn me on!
:)

I'm finishing off a cd cover for a great local musician. Quark Kent, he does sort of groovy electronic, space cocktail music. He's got previous releases in the shops so yawl should really go out and buy the whole collection!

My inspirations are pretty much anything really. The standard crop of Swiss designers and Latte fiends! But seriously, everything around me, friends and peers that inspire me, music, things other than design books and magazines etc.

My tips:
mmm, be persistent.
believe in yourself.
take it easy and wear a clean pair of undies every day with one day being "sexy undie day".

One more thing:
a little empathy goes a long way.


Duncan's work features in the Saturday 20th October edition of ABC's noise tv, 10.30 am - 11.30 am, repeated Saturday night before 'Rage'.
