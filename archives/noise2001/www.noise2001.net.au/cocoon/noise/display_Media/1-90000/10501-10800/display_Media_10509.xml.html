Mixing wise, I'd have to say I was inspired by...

the 'Monkey Mafia live at the Heavenly Social' mix CD by Jon Carter. When I first heard it a few years ago it changed the way I mixed records. Although it isn't exactly ground breaking mixing and contains little
Hip-Hop and zero scratching it got me interested in Ragga and Dancehall, motivated me to explore the use of accapellas over beats and proved that you don't always have to do long, drawn out beat mixes.

More recently I'd have to mention everyones favourite mix tape of the moment 'Brainfreeze' by DJ Shadow and Cut Chemist. What intrigues me the most about this mix
- aside from the seamless manner in which it was constructed, the track selection and the shear fact that they own multiple copies of these rare gems - is thinking about the time they must have spent practicing and refining it. When you watch the live video of this mix you truly appreciate how amazing it is!

From the Mix Up special I hope to get feedback from people who enjoy it - that and the opportunity to play interstate.

You can expect to hear lots of Hip-Hop (especially Aussie) with a pinch of Ragga and Deep Funk.

'How would you describe your DJ style?'
All over the shop.

A great 90 minutes session of dance radio to me is an hour and a half of no interuptions, a clear frequency and a healthy serving of local talent.

My track of the moment is Breakestra 'Cramp Your Style' (all 2.5 mins of it!)

My favourite DJs:
Cut Chemist
DJ Shadow
DJ Design
Rob Life
DJ Noise
Nick Toth

My tips:
Keep it interesting. Try and cram in as much stuff as you possibly can and don't limit yourself to just one style of music. Chuck in an intro, a sample from your favourite movie or whatever. Try and personalise your mix as much as possible and only use tracks that you
love - never compromise your tastes. Oh, and only ever use vinyl - CD's are for suckers!

Keep your eyes peeled for the soon to be released Ninety Calibre clothing range, new music from The Hospice MC's and Canberra in general!

Shout outs to Tink, FourSE, Hospice MC's, Tone Def and especially Yass (cheers for lending us your 06 mate!)
