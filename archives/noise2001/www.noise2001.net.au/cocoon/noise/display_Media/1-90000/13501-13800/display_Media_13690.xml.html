DESIGNATED


This week I'm going to stray from film and talk about teen drinking, parties and my own "journey" if you will last night.
Ever since Smashed I've assumed I've known to an extent what a party is, which now that I've been to one and not drunk, I've realised how wrong I was. Last night was Smashed on steroids, and I was smack bang in the very middle of it. My friend Nezmia passed out early, which was not good because I had promised her boyfriend that I'd take care of her, but I was more preoccupied with Katie -- my best friend. Triggering her own self-inflicted spiral to oblivion my night soon became entirely based around looking after her. Two guys who will remain nameless tried to take advantage of her -- one persisting over and over again. Thankfully, she lost her $150 custom made bracelet -- getting them out of the picture for a while -- crying her eyes out we retraced her steps. At numerous moments I felt like ramming my fingers down her throat and making her vomit, my night was spent making sure hers went without a hitch. I asked for the torch, we found the bracelet, I kept her safe from the people trying to...well, you know. After the bracelet I ran into Beth -- long story -- and the whole night seemed perfectly worth it. I left at the same time as Katie, who was still pretty drunk, but God...I was lucky that I had made it. Then someone spiked Alli's drink. Tom was off his head. Nez was on the ground. I was standing [holding Katie up] in the middle of a dark and hazy world of drugs and booze. I felt like William Miller with Penny Lane in that New York hotel, hopelessly alone.
Today I've just told a story. I hope it made you think.
If not, well, it just proves what I'm talking about.
