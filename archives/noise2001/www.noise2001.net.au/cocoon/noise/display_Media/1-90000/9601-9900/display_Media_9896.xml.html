name  // Pan Sapien's 'Black Lodge' remix of Powderfinger's 'The Metre'  
creator  // Andrew Perry 
project  //REMIX 
The 'concept' of this remix is loosely based around a mythical place in 'Twin Peaks' - the Black Lodge.


It supposedly comes from Native American mythology, and is a place a bit like Purgatory I think, full of evil spirits and stolen souls. In the Black Lodge the characters appear to 'talk backwards', and time is distorted
(4-dimensional almost . .:)) . . . I tried to get this feel into the track, with lots of 'disembodied' voices & reverse-echo-reverse effects to get the talking backwards bit happening.

Afterwards, I realised (and was told by others) I'd probably been heavily influenced by the two latest Radiohead albums (KidA & Insomniac), but any similarities in sound were unintentional. I most enjoyed working with the vocals. Unlike many 'rock' vocals, the vocal in this track are so crisp they can stand up on their own right. In part of the track I stripped back most sounds and let the vocals almost solo . . . I'm almost regretting putting too many effects on them now since they were so beutiful to begin with.

I also found some amusing obscenities muttered by Powderfingers drumming
(sorry forgot his name) at the end of the drum track. This was an opportunity for naughty sampling I couldn't pass up, so I worked it in. . . (well, I hope it 'worked' :))

I was trying to 'change everything' in the original track, but without making it like a whole new track with just a few short samples cut the original (like many remixes). Not that just cutting a few samples and using them alone is a bad thing. . . I just wanted to bring it into the Black Lodge style as much as I could without just doing a new track and calling it a remix.

I added strings from my old 8-bit Ensoniq Mirage sampling keyboard, and a deep jungle/techno bass drum sample made with Stomper. I think everything else was in some way taken from the original track, albeit cut up messed around and effected to buggerey, so it may be hard to tell just what it was originally.

Tech Stuff// For this remix, I used CoolEditPro for the main mix, Buzz tracker for applying some effects, Fruityloops for making the drumloops, StomperUltra for a bass drum sample. I also used my Ensoniq Mirage sampling keyboard. Software was run on a AMD K7 900, 256Mb RAM, SBLive card, 30Gb HDD. But do not despair if you don't have a beefy computer... I used to do similar things on a Pentium 233Mhz, 64 Mb RAM & a 5 Gb HDD. (plus copious amounts of caffiene contributing to a non-linear chaotic process)

Fav Remixes// I like the way Nine Inch Nails releases a remix album to complement their 'real' albums. While its hard to choose a favorite, I quite like the 'starfuckers inc (version)' remix (both tracks 6 and 10) on 'things falling apart' (remixes from 'the fragile').

Tips// While it's easy to get instant results from a lot of the software available today, take the time to learn the ins-and-outs of some of the less used or more difficult to use software. It will pay off when everyone else 'sounds like Rebirth' and you sound original.


Andrew is a finalist of the noise Remix project. Pan Sapien's 'Black Lodge' remix of Powderfinger's 'The Metre' can be heard on triple j through out the month of October.
