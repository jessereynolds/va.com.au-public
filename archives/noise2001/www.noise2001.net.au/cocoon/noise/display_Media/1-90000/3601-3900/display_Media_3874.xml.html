name  // From the Next Room 
creator  // Laura Vines 
project  //TRUE TALES OF LOVE AND HATE 
A poem written from the perspective of a child overhearing their parents fighting, this piece is both sensitive and raw. It has been described as more of a "screech than a poem", but it is not a cliched, self-indulgent work describing an angst-ridden teen. Rather, it is a compelling evocation of human despair.

--

FROM THE NEXT ROOM

Like glass splintering through calmed air,
Their voices slice the silence.
Soaking through the walls, anger and
Despair flood my ears.
As a great weight falling from a huge height
Their intensity grows and grows.
It gathers momentum and culminates in one
Overwhelming summit of scream.

I cannot comprehend their words,
but the sheer volume of their shrieking is enough.
Their shrill voices shiver the air.
I think I can see their vocal chords, stretched to snapping point,
Like guitar strings, their fibres gradually fraying.
Words are not needed. Meaning is packed tight into
The sound of their teary voices. As if it had a monetary value.
There is not even room enough for breath.

I listen and I imagine how they look.
I can almost see the shiny lacquer of their hearts
Crack and crease. I can virtually witness the
Vessels break and the eerie silence that follows.
I wait. I wait in the spiky silence, like
I am bracing myself for a blow.
I wait, a buffer between them.
Maybe they have finished. Maybe they will let

Each other go. But they are both bleeding now
And soon its flow will draw them back.
