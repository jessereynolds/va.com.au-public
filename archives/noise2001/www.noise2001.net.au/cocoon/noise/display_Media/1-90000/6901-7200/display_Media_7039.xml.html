name  // Tacky Atari 
creator  // Gideon Cheok 
project  //FLASH ANIMATION 
If you were a child of 80s you can understand the place in my heart where...

I keep dear memories of Astro Boy, Michael Jackson and Aero Sport shoes. These things were cool - well in Primary school at least along with the popular (and additive) video games from Atari.
"Atari?" I hear you say. Yes the company responsible for bringing us games where frogs went over logs and where parachuters climbed onto one another√s shoulders in order to blow up a shooting tower. I see that I√ve jogged your memory.
In order to construct our tribute to Atari my partner and I wanted to create a 80s Atari feel without actually copying any imagery from Atari games. This forced us to use pixels in our images along with the application of bright, garish colours. You will also notice that the animation is also very ëlow-tech√ which brings us to our other objective for this animation.
Both my partner and I wanted to challenge the way users see Flash. Being both Multimedia Design students, we understand that in our high-tech world users have now got the opportunity to create sophisticated, rendered animations. Nevertheless this style is not necessary when using Flash. Our animation strives to not only conjure up the feel of a 80s Atari game but to also show that through using Flash, simple yet effective animations can be achieved.
