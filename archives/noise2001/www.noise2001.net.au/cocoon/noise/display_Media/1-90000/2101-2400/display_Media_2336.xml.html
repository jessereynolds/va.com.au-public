name  // Consumer God 
creator  // John Bergin 
project  //JUICE MAGAZINE 
OIL PASTELS, PENCIL ON PAPER DIGITALLY MANIPULATED 11 cm x 13.42 cm (print)

The clothes, the car, the cosmopolitan lifestyle, the swanky accessories. We want them, need them, just gotta have them. Society has become infatuated with the collection and disposal of possessions, beliefs, and emotions. We are consumer junkies, getting our fix on a daily basis. It seems only fitting, therefore, that a shrine to this physical/mental reliance to consumerism be erected and worshipped: a mishmash of contemporary material values and pseudo-religious conviction. Well dressed, seductive, and with all the latest mod-cons, this satirical homage suggests that spiritual depth can be attained through commercialism, often serving as a means of distracting ourselves from the mechanised, formulaic nature of our own lives. But escapism comes at a price: "Caveat Emptor" (Let The Buyer Beware).
