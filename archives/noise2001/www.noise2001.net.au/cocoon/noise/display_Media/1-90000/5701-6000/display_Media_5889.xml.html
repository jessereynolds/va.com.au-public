name  // 1/3 - Sledde Chair 
creator  // John Ellway 
project  //MONUMENT MAGAZINE 
Object Description // The inspiration for this chair came from the notion and challenge of creating a design that looked uncomfortable and impossible to interact with, but in fact proved to be quite the opposite.

The impossibility of the curved sitting surface was overcome by the use of a material that adapted to the user's body shape and weight. The strapping that makes up the Sledde Chair consists of variably stretched elastic. Elastic in the area of the buttocks is less tense than where the feet and head contact the chair's surface. This allows the user to sink into the curved surface without falling off.

While the chair is large, its transparent nature allows it to integrate with its surrounding environment. The chair's metal frame would be made of tubular steel and be manufactured such that the chair would be assembled from four pieces and the strapping clip onto the frame.

A person sitting on the chair would sit in a semi-reclined position with their head and feet supported by the elastic strapping allowing the body a floating sensation, suspended away from any surface. //
