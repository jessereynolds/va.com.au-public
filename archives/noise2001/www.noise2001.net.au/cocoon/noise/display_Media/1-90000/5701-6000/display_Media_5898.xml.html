name  // 3/3 - Rill Lamp 
creator  // John Ellway 
project  //MONUMENT MAGAZINE 
Object Description // The 'Rill' table lamp was inspired by the exoskeleton of millipede type organisms. The segmented nature of the millipede is carried through the design by the thirty circular plastic ribs that are hinged on the central 'spine' of the design. This central spine is the 'light source' of the table lamp and has the potential to be made in two forms.

The first being a length of 'side emitting' fibre optic cable with the light source being at one end, allowing a 'flex' in the spine of the light. This allows the user to customise its shape. This form of light comes with the expense of the fibre optic cable. The other form of the spine uses a fixed shape neon tube like the ones seen in neon signage, reducing the cost.

The idea of the clear plastic spines are to emit spines of light. The light emitted from the spine flow out the ends of the ribs. This design has the potential to be taken into other configurations including an upright lamp, garden lighting and roof lighting. //
