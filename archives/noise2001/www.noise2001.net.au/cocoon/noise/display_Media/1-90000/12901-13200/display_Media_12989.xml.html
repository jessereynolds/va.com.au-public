Centipede spends a moment with noise:

Inspiration?//

is everywhere

For the song?//

it's about a deep relationship that didn't turn out the way i'd planned, as things sometimes don't. But oten that's a more rewarding process to go through, even though it's seemingly more difficult at the time.

Generally?//

beautiful souls, any sound that's interesting

Any particular production techniques used for the recording of the track?//

This track was quite traditional in it's production for us. some of our other songs have more complex production tecniques.

Type of equipment used?//

We record at our home studio. this song has alot of live instrumentation using guitars, bass, as well as samples. we wish that we had audio gear that was a little more fancy, but at this stage, it seems to suffice.

Favourite local artists?//

There are some awesome sydney bands at present.
decoder ring, ukiyo e, the devoted few, esn
plus too many australian bands to mention, but here's a few more:
underground lovers, adam said galore, deloris, ninetynine and on and on!

Favourite artists of all time?//

a difficult question. Swervedriver, bjork, art of noise, nirvana, lisa germano, pixies.....
there are too many.

Any tips for people undertaking their first recording session?//

take your time! wait until you can afford to spend a decent amount of time in the studio, and your experience will be more worthwhile.

Did you ultimately achieve the result that you wanted?//

Ultimately, yes. we are very pleased with our results, considering it's a first release.

Ultimate goal/aspiration?//

Keep making music that hopefully some people get to hear and enjoy.

Anything else that you would like to mention?//

Anyone can play guitar


Centipede's 'Ptb' can also be heard on the Making noise CD - free with the October's Rolling Stone magazine.
