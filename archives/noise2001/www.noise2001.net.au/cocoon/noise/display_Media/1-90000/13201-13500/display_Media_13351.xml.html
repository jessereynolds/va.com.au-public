name  // I Miss You 
creator  // Kate Williams 
project  //TRUE TALES OF LOVE AND HATE 
This is a story about when you have to leave someone because being with them makes you hurt too much. but you still love them.

--

"Please remember me and smile,
'Cause it's better to forget,
Than to remember me and cry"
-The Cure 'Treasure'

He remembers her birthday, and the ceremonious present giving he carried out. How she shrieked at the sight of his presents and couldn't stop kissing him and jumping around like a child. He remembers before she went away for so long, when they stumbled around the streets all night, drank cider on some anonymous front porch, climbed into the Vinnie bin on Butterworth street, and then, apon arriving home, ate cheese and onion sandwiches. He remembers when she got her new red converse shoes and she wrote rude words on them and decided to sleep in them that night, only to take them off a few hours later, complaining of cold toes.

He thinks about their sharing of obsessions, how they trampled over each other time and time again, but always just made it all right again by poking out their tongues. They sang Depeche Mode together, they tried to scare each other by screaming "Dare!", and they climbed in her window even though she had the keys to the door. They rolled about on the grass and he told her: "You should have a tinsel crown and fairy wand... you chase me around and tiny sparks fly out of your wand and make me fall over÷and I just crawl to you", and swept a blade of grass from her cheek.

She thinks about just before she had to go away for a long time, how seeing him made her sigh and hold her hands to her chest, and apon closing her eyes, found she couldn't open them again.

And the last time they were together?

It was raining, and they sat in the park. He held her hand and she cried. And when it was time to go she left first. She sucks on a ping pong ball she found in the bottom of his Guinness can in remembrance of him.
