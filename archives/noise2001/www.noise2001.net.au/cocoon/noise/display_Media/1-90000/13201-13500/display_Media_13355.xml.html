name  // 'Young Murri Women Singin' It Up', 4AAA, Queensland. 
creator  // Belinda Miller 
project  //COMMUNITY RADIO 
Showcasing the immense talent that Kerrianne Cox, Rochelle Watson and The Stiff Gins have to offer to their communities and the rest of Australia.

Singing and storytelling is an integral part of Aboriginal culture. It is a part of our upbringing and way of life. This documentary is a combination of music and interviews. We hear these three indigenous women sing from their heart, we listen to their songs that are about Aboriginal issues that affect women and portray their constant strength to deal with the injustices of the past, and everyday living of young Aboriginal women. They speak about their culture, spirituality, family and identity and how all of this has influenced and inspired the music they produce. We hear young Murri women singin' it up.

Singing is a way of communication, education and reconciliation, particularly within the context of Aboriginal culture. It is important that we realise that these Murri women have something to offer the music industry, however due to little or no recognition it is a struggle to get their music played and their stories heard.


Catch 'Young Murri Women Singin' It Up' on 4AAA, 98.9FM Queensland, throughout October.

In conjunction with Arts Queensland, as part of the Queensland Government's cultural policy for young people, noise has commissioned $20,000 worth of radio production by young Queensland radio producers for the festival.
