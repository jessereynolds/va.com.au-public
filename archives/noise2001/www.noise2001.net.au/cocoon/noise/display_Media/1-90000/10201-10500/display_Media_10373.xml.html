My most recent work involves constructing a room that resembles a cabin..
or hunting lodge, filled with the spoils of the hunt. The trophies are constructed from a range of materials, but are mainly paper mache over a wire armature which is then painted, covered in flock or fake fur and given features like teeth, claws and plastic eyes. In a macabre twist the imitation taxidermy is often worked into a functional object, for example there is the owl ashtray, wolf drink holder and hedgehog candlestick holders.

As well as satisfying my desire to make crafty things, this work is an exploration of my interest in the aesthetics of the North American and European forest and woodlands. Having grown up in the Australian forest, I find images of places like the Alps and Yellowstone National Park so much more interesting and exotic than what is in my own back yard. The work also suggests ideas about how humans want to relate to animal world, particularly our efforts to always be in control of nature.
