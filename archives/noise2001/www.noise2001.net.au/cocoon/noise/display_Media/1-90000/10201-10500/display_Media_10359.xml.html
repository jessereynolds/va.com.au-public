name  // spareparts industries 
creator  // ange foong 
project  //SBS 
clothing label : spareparts laboratories
lifespan : since february 2001... and still operating.

owners: three sisters in newcastle, Rowena 23, Juliana 21 and Angela 18 yrs old.
we stock other local labels : hooley australia, chris johnson, fusnitch, pandemonium designs, the cat's pyjamas, foxie designs, fletch, lucas sales, kirky, betty, huckleberry.

some series of clothing :
BLUE MURDER (boys blue collar blood stained prints). ELIZABETH, VICTORIA AND DIANA (frilled english collar tops for chicks). THE PANTS' PANTS (androgynous kung-fu pants with the word pants printed on them). AUNTY WOO GOES WEST (bright pleated girls' tops with panels of chinese floral prints). FRONT SIDE IS BOTH SIDES (tops that can be worn on either side). A BORING SHIRT (plaid and plain coloured collared guys' shirts with ribbon of stitching "a boring shirt") ...and more to come.

others :
we have in the works, designing uniforms for local businesses : aprons for Spanish couch cafe called "Salar" : uniforms for a new winebar/cafe opening in our street : hairshows : clothing for bands/singers : collaborating with Dept. of Design at University of Newcastle to use our shopfront as an assignment for commercial merchandising : organising exhibitions with other independent self-employed designers : silly fashion shows

our shop :
we started with 13 cast iron clothes racks and a shitload of '90's secondhand clothes. on a budget of $500 we scoured scrap yards, dump sites, demolition yards, people's backyards, recycling bins, garage sales and refurbished the shop to make it look "spareparts industries".

our exhibition:
called "shelf life" - in conjunction with four other local designers (graphic designer, photographer, industrial designer and architect as well as our garments) which will be opened on Friday 28th September at 7pm by Elizabeth Anne McGregor form the Museum of Contemporary Arts, held for two weeks from the 28th at a space located above Caf?© Plumes in Hunter St Mall, Newcastle (7A Wolfe St, Newcastle)

our passion and inspiration:
we like to sew, create and design. The opportunity arose to be able to open our own business and we took it. With limited finance and plenty of ideas we thought it would be great experience to expose our designs and give other local designers the opportunity to display theirs. Inspired by our customers and all the people around us who constantly support us and encourage our creativity. The fact that they like to wear our clothes makes us keep going.

Realistically, we don't place too much emphasis on our concepts and we usually just take an inkling of an idea and interpret it somehow through clothing. We rarely stick to any plans. Sewing depends highly on our moods and it is often reflected in what results.

We're constantly learning new techniques, especially through other clothesmakers we meet. In fact, we feed off the creativity of our consignees by talking and appreciating their skills which inspires and pushes us to think of more un-ordinary ideas. People inspire us. Apathy inspires us. Mundanity is also a great source of inspiration. They are all abundant and persistent and ensures we will never be lost for ideas.
