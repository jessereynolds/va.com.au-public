blurb  // I am one of three sisters in newcastle, who form spareparts industries. I am 18 years old.

spareparts industries is a clothing label : spareparts laboratories, and a shop in newcastle where we sell our own-made clothes and other funky labels, at 74 darby street.

we started with 13 cast iron clothes racks and a shitload of '90's secondhand clothes. on a budget of $500 we scoured scrap yards, dump sites, demolition yards, people's backyards, recycling bins, garage sales and refurbished the shop to make it look "spareparts industries".

lifespan : since february 2001 and still operating

we scout for fabrics far and wide, mostly use off cuts gathered from opp shops reverse garbage markets...

Rowena was the first one to be interested in fashion design, Julianna followed suit and then I did. I am studying economics and commerce.

we are all self trained in making clothes, trial and error kind of girls. we built our skills up from the basics - our mum taught us...

we have in the works, designing uniforms for local businesses : aprons for Spanish couch cafe called "Salar" : uniforms for a new winebar/cafe opening in our street : hairshows : clothing for bands/singers : collaborating with Dept. of Design at University of Newcastle to use our shopfront as an assignment for commercial merchandising : organising exhibitions with other independent self-employed designers : silly fashion shows

our exhibition:
called "shelf life" - in conjunction with four other local designers (graphic designer, photographer, industrial designer and architect as well as our garments) which will be opened on Friday 28th September at 7pm by Elizabeth Anne McGregor form the Museum of Contemporary Arts, held for two weeks from the 28th at a space located above Cafe Plumes in Hunter St Mall, Newcastle (7A Wolfe St, Newcastle)
