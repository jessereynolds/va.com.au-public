blurb  // I create photographic based imagery incorporating textures and natural elements..

using collage with analogue and digital manipulation to make print based representations. The majority of my work is very peaceful, with a sense of atmosphere, calmness and beauty. One of the strongest underlying influences is that of the Blue Mountains, my home ... texture, organic line and form, the rhythms of the seasons.

My inspirations lie in the world that surrounds us ... little things can become the greatest pictures. It is all in the way we view our own surroundings and what we are willing to see, absolutely everything has the potential to be a great image or to inspire one. There is no wrong answer in self expression.

My art making processes are a culmination of many epic years of fine arts and design training and my own curiosity. All my work is photography based and plays with the layering of image and texture. My approach is hands on, the computer is the last point in the artistic process. The more imagery and textures generated outside of the computer the better, as the results are more original.

I love the grain of film and the inexactness of hand made. I also find that I get a greater sense of 'creating' if I can touch and experiment with its development manually before it is digitally pushed into its end form.

What deters me from reaching for the stars...
....is confidence in my own work

Not in the creating or doing but in the pitching and selling aspects of art and design, all the things they never teach you at Uni! Trying to find a balance between commercial and personal work and putting a monetary value to the things that I create, I can find this VERY daunting. My work lies in that hazy area between Design and Art, with photography being the linking thread.

The diverse directions and mediums of my work has made finding a focus, where all those elements come together, a challenge. However through finding out how it all fits together and having the confidence to say 'this is what I love to do, this is my work!' I have come to see that I do not have to fit a set career name. I create images in many different forms, my work can and does sit between the lines. I just have more to offer, varied skills and a different perspective.

More of my work is available or any questions, please contact me.
