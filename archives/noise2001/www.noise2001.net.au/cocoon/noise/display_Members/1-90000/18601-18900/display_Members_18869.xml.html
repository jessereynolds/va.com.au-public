blurb  // Some stuff about me . . .

I have completed a Bachelor of Arts (Design) (Hons) at Curtin University of Technology, Western Australia in 1999, majoring in 3-Dimensional Studies (Industrial Design).

Since my graduation and during my Honours year, I have had some professional experience as a freelance designer working on a variety of projects.

I have worked as an illustrator for Corporate Theatre Productions executing visuals and renderings of stage and set designs. I have redesigned logos and letterheads for TADWA (Technical Aid to the Disabled WA) as a volunteer and have done some work (for a disability consultant working for the Department of Transport in Western Australia) preparing and drafting plans of tactile ground treatments for several metropolitan railway stations around Perth. I have also had some experience designing and developing a website for Westralian Technical Consultants (www.westraliantech.com.au)

The majority of my time in the year 2000 has been taken up mostly working closely with two other Curtin graduates (both of whom have business and marketing backgrounds) developing Idea Generation - a product design and development company, designing and developing our own products for manufacture.

Apart from that, I haven't done much, except sit around and think about what kind of designer I want to be, and what kind of design do I want to do.

One quote I recently read by Ettore Sottsass (famous Italian designer):

"What I wanted was to finally decide between design and industrial design. They involve different areas of responsibility. Designers have responsibility towards people. Industrial design is only obliged to the company, to the market. I hardly do any more industrial design. We only work with companies who have a feeling of responsibility for our society."

I guess I just want to do design that helps people, engages and provokes thought and enriches people's lives.
