blurb  // Artist of the Day, 2/10/01

recently having gained the confidence to consider myself a graphic and visual artist.

- hopefully without qualms or much pretence. i suppose the confidence comes from having achieved a standard that i am happy with, due to lots of practice.

i have a diploma of graphic design (which i did so i could learn mostly about the computer software and get lots of drawing practice). i did my course at TAFE in brisbane; i bitched a lot about it, but i learned heaps in two years. i finished in dec 2000.

aside from that, what largely shapes me as a person and my vision of the world and creativity is my family history. i was born in england, but since 1985 i have been an australian citizen (we moved here in '83 - when i thought dionne warwick and easy listening music was "cool").

at the beginning of WWII, all four of my grandparents were forcibly removed from their homes in the ukraine and made to labour in work camps in germany for the next five years. when the war ended, they were accepted by england as refugees. poor, most definitely tired, confused and unable to speak english, they all had a bit of a difficult life. consequently, those difficulties resulted in much anxiety with the upbringing of my parents, particularly my mother who committed suicide in 1993, just before my 18th birthday.

i understand why she did it, and i accept her action as a course of life, but what has shaped my understanding of humanity from my experiences is that feelings are not to be taken at face value and that it's okay to feel whatever you feel, but jeez it's hard work being honest about these things!...but don't give up because anything is possible.

i'd love to be paid for my artwork (digital and 'analogue'). at the moment i am an office cleaner, the industry is slow to employ graduates it seems... i'd love to do graphics/art work for the entertainment industries: music, films, fashion; that kind of thing - anything that makes a relevant and intelligent and/or confronting statement about the world in which we live (i kinda like confrontation).

influences since i was young: definitely davida allen, andy warhol, joan miro, ralph steadman. more recently: graham rounthwaite, jeffrey smart and gordon bennett; plus some of my friends: james bailey, ben lawrance, sara shera, simone tait and matthew doddrell for his amazing (some may think stupid) ideas.

how do i do what i do? i love photoshop and my scanner and graphology and typography. i take photographs, i pick up rubbish from the street and gutters and waste paper bins. i read lots (these days) and look at heaps of stuff and spend a bunch of time on my own. i keep visual diaries and write down anything interesting that comes into my head. i like chalk and sticker graffiti.

i like riding my bike. i don't have a licence (i tried 3 times for my auto, but failed each one). i think this affects the way i see things; at a pace that is more determined by other people instead of me and 'my car' charging thru with fewer limitations; something to that effect.

i paint with acrylic and ink and use a variety of brushes and i like to use my hands a lot. i think i'm starting to get into pharmaceutical chemicals like mercurchrome and potassium (which i understand can set paper alight if it's not diluted enough).

the only gripe pertaining to my artwork is not at the moment being able to travel to see more of life to enrich my understanding and my creativity.

i do what i do because i don't like the image of life being projected largely by deadline-pressured artwork, created in an environment which is too busy to be accurately reflective of reality (too much of time is money these days). advertising is overtaking culture at an alarming rate.

finally, everyone has problems and i think a lot of people are not listened to, and they end up learning not being able to listen to themselves or others. the power of money is so much more suffocating these days. i aim to challenge that.

where can people see more of my stuff?? if you're in melbourne from the 21st of may, i'm having an exhibition of works titled "FASHION GONE WRONG" at fat ben's cafe: centre place arcade near flinders st in the city.
