blurb  // I'm 21 years old and live in a share house in Surry Hills.

I live with four other people who used to be my friends (only joking).

I'll spare you the details: it's mostly boring, and when you're not bored, you're scared.

When I'm not reading, writing, drawing, painting, working, watching movies, brainstorming, hanging out at the local cafe, procrastinating, experimenting, daydreaming, dabbling in web design and snapping photos, I sleep.

My artwork is fashioned using materials such as acrylic and oil-based paints, oil crayons, ink, pencil and charcoal, as well as various other mixed media (basically whatever I get my hands on). Programs such as Adobe Photoshop are then used to digitise, enhance and manipulate parts of my artwork until I get the desired effect.

I am constantly seeking to evolve and explore stylistically and creatively, learning new programs and experimenting feverishly, eager to push beyond classical print aesthetics and preconceptions.

Ultimately, I wish to pursue a career in graphic design / advertising / visual communication. At present, I am greatly influenced by the Pop Art and Surrealist movements; the works of Marcel Duchamp and Jackson Pollock; the writing of Douglas Coupland and resident artists such as Greg Egan and Reg Mombassa. Gritty, industrial techniques of mass-production, such as silk screen printing, linocut and graffiti art also characterise my work.

So, there you have it.
