blurb  // I live for Radio!

It's something that I always wanted to be a part of and it is my career goal to work at a Professional Radio Station. (I didn't write Commercial for a reason:) So, I became one of the Presenters at the Community Radio Station here in Mackay and I haven't looked back, I love it... can't get enough of it!

Being a presenter here doesn't just mean going and doing your program and going home again. We love getting involved in the station and other Community projects.

Crazy activities include: Bowling in your Pyjamas, Hosting Rock Pop Mime nights and dancing to the Archies.

There are so many other things I love about this station: The people, the programs - the request ones are always great, although the Slim Dusty album always seems to mysteriously disappear just before the show starts... :) And of course, the music!!

I have pretty much grown up in Mackay since I was 4. I started learning music then - piano and percussion and a few years later Flute. P.s. I have managed to play Pomp and Circumstance in Orchestras every year since grade 6, I know four different versions off by heart and I am so not proud of that:)

Currently I am studying a BA Maj. Multimedia/ Communication at Central Queensland University in Mackay. (This my third year as a first year - I'm one of those perpetual students who loves Uni. too much to graduate:) Hopefully, next year I will be studying a Radio course in Sydney, wish me luck:)

My inspirations and influences would have to be from my best friends (I know its soppy). Some of them I have been friends with for 16 years, for others it just feels that way:)

Leanne (the other half of this project) and I have almost completed our submission and that is a Four Part Radio Documentary on the plethora of musical talent in Mackay. It should be airing soon so watch out for it: Project Yah Mackay!: on 4CRM

In conjunction with Arts Queensland, as part of the Queensland Government's cultural policy for young people, noise has commissioned $20,000 worth of radio production by young Queensland radio producers for the festival.

Tune in to community radio stations across Queensland for 'noise live' special programs, featuring local young artists, and listen out for pre-produced features throughout the month.
