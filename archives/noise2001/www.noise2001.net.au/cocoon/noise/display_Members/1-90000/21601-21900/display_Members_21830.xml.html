FAVOURITE COMIC
I read quite a lot of comics and have many favourites. I could not put down Maus by Art Spiegelman and Joe Sacco's Palestine and Soba. I also enjoyed The Amazing True Story of a Teenage Mum by Katherine Arnoldi and I like Kochalka. Another recent interest is What's Michael by Makoto Kobayashi and I like the art and stories of Adrian Tomine.

Out of these, Joe would have to be my favourite artist. For those who don't know his work, Joe Sacco is a very talented artist and journalist who has drawn comic books about places such as Palestine and Bosnia. He goes to the places to interview people and then draws their stories. I have worked with refugees from both these countries and have found his insight through comics extremely valuable in understanding the experiences of the people I have met. Truly, I find his work educational and moving. I remember some of the things he's drawn and think "Did I see that on a documentary?" and then remember it was his work. He is unbelievable and I've made other non-comic readers read his comics because they are so unreal.

COMICS FOR GIRLS
It annoys me that there is such a small range of comics made with women in mind. My nine-year old niece has recently become obsessed with comics (Archie, Sabrina, etc) but I guess she will lose interest as she grows up and finds there isn't much for her age-group and gender. I don't think there is enough for girls. Have you looked in any comic shops recently?

THE CREATIVE PROCESS
The hardest thing about drawing comics is deciding what pictures will convey the right meaning for what you are trying to say.

It feels exciting to have drawn a comic. I can't believe it. I would like to try to do it again but I feel my ideas and stories are much better than my drawings. I would like to draw a longer comic about my experiences working in the Woomera Detention Centre last year, but I think it will be very difficult.

The best thing is looking at the finished product and thinking, "My God, I did that!!"
