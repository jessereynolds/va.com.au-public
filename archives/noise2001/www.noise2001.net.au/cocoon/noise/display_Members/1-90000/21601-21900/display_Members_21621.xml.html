blurb  // Otherwise known as the artist 'Pan sapiens'.

I try to determine protein structures by day and make nasty-weird electronic music by night. I've been doing electronic music since I was in about Year 12, which is about 5 years ago.

WARNING INCOMING PLUG: I have music on the web at www.mp3.com/pansapiens - check it out if you want.

My original influences are in epic rock bands like The Smashing Pumpkins, Pink Floyd and U2(and Micheal Jackson 'Thriller', and the 'War of the Worlds' musical record). I've also been heavily influenced by Black Lung (Sydney electronic artist David Thrussel), Tetraphnm (Jeremy Dower, Melbourne), Regurgitator, Nine Inch Nails, The Cure, Bi(f)tek, Tool, Beck and more recently Radiohead.

I LOVE the Lucksmiths. If I were a girl I'd have a lovechild with all of them ( :) ).
I also really like Tetraphnm (Jeremy Dower, Melbourne), Bi(f)tek, Darren Verhagen (shinjuku filth/thief), and a guy who used to be on mp3.com, now planetmg.com called Skrooloose. And my friends 'The Movies', a happy 60's-ish pop band. Also 'bloq' from Sydney (I think). I could go on and on.

I know people often whinge that there are limited opportunities for publishing / performance. I think as long as you don't want to make money, publishing is easy since the internet became big (see mp3.com, mp3.com.au, besonic.com the list goes on). If you want to make money out of your music, then it's much more difficult. I get the feeling that for PC based musicians like myself, getting a live gig would be very hard, but I've honestly never tried so I shouldn't comment.

5 Albums that shook the world//
The Wall, Pink Floyd.
Wish, The Cure.
The Downward Spiral, Nine Inch Nails.
War of the Worlds, can't remember. Album-musical from the 70's.
Machina - The Machines of God, Smashing Pumpkins (why does no-one listen to this!!)
